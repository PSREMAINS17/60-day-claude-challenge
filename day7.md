Profile analysis.
As a heavy-user student focused on coding, research, and career preparation, you need a setup that delivers consistent quality across a high volume of daily tasks without exhausting your usage limits too quickly.
That combination — high frequency plus varied task types — makes model and effort discipline especially important.

Why Sonnet is your primary model. 
Sonnet sits at the sweet spot between intelligence and efficiency.
It handles complex code debugging, algorithm explanations, and research summaries with genuine depth, while being fast enough to use repeatedly throughout the day.

Opus is more powerful, but using it for routine tasks would drain your limits for the moments that truly matter — system design questions, interview prep, or your capstone project.

When to escalate. 
The clearest signal to move up to Opus + Max effort is when the stakes are high and the reasoning is deep: coding interviews, architecture decisions, or any deliverable that directly affects your academic or career outcomes. For everything else, Sonnet at Standard or High effort is the right call.

The one habit that will make the biggest difference. When submitting a coding problem, always include the full error message, the relevant code block, and a description of what you expected to happen. This single practice will dramatically improve the quality and accuracy of Claude's responses, regardless of which model you use.
